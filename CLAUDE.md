## My Product & Market

**What problem does my product solve?**
My work product helps my team members quickly, efficiently and correctly assemble veteran's health and services records to package up evidence that they have considerable injuries and are entitled to disability compensation from the VA (Veteran's Administration)
My personal product helps homeowners compare different contractor proposals and evaluate and ultimately select the right proposal.

**Who are my users?**
For my work products - Operations people, Nurses, Lawyers, Paralegals and Medical Assistants.
For Comparezy - Primary: Homeowners ("Hallie Homeowner" — time-constrained, risk-averse, cost-conscious, not a construction expert). Secondary: real estate investors, small landlords, property managers. Long-term enterprise targets: insurance carriers, home warranty providers, lenders (HELOC/mortgage).

**Who are my main competitors?**
For my work - There are smaller firms, Cannon Disability Law, Grundy Disability group.  (mostly law firms)
For Comparezy - No direct competitor owns the proposal evaluation space. Adjacent competitors are lead-gen platforms (Angi, Thumbtack, Houzz, AllBetter, Bark.com, HomeGuide) — but they stop at contractor connection and don't help evaluate bids. The most important non-obvious competitor is AI chat (ChatGPT/Claude at $20/month) — tech-savvy homeowners already upload quotes manually. Comparezy's counter: structured output, local benchmarks, no prompt engineering required, mobile-first, shareable reports.

**What are the most important metrics for my product?**
For my work - across our tech stack, the claims and dollar amounts won.
For Comparezy - Projects evaluated, conversion rate at second quote upload (key trigger), revenue. Long-term: data volume for enterprise benchmarking.

**What stage is my company/product?**
For my work - bootstraped (never got funding, but 100 Million Revenue)
For Comparezy - Solo founder, pre-launch. Finishing product before marketing/distribution. Revenue model: freemium + $19–$49/project (Phase 1) → subscriptions (Phase 2) → enterprise data intelligence (Phase 3). Target: ~$500K Year 2, ~$1.6M Year 3.
