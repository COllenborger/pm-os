## Recent Competitor Moves (Last 90 Days)

- **Angi:** Launched the Angi app *inside ChatGPT* (March 2026) — homeowners who ask ChatGPT a home project question can now hire directly through Angi without leaving the chat. This is the most significant competitive move in the market. Angi is treating AI chat as a **distribution channel**, not a threat. Also continuing 2026 AI strategy: AI Helper (LLM-guided service requests), proprietary leads growth, improved pro retention. Revenue declining but margins improving after 12% workforce cuts.
- **Thumbtack:** No major product announcements. Lead quality still deteriorating — contractors report $35–$200+ per lead with low conversion; refunds issued as platform credits, not cash. The paid-lead model broadly described as "collapsing" in contractor marketing communities as Google LSA takes share.
- **AllBetter:** No disclosed funding rounds or major product updates. Still positioning around escrow + vetted contractors. 27K contractors, 30K homeowners on platform — early stage.
- **HomeGuide:** Updated 2026 cost guides across project categories — still the best pre-bid cost research tool. No decision-support features added. No product announcements.
- **Houzz:** Upgraded the Houzz Pro Client Dashboard (2026) with project schedules, daily progress updates, 3D floor plans, invoices, and in-project messaging. This is about **active project management**, not proposal comparison — deepening contractor-to-homeowner communication during construction, not evaluation beforehand.
- **AI Chat (ChatGPT/Claude):** No contractor-quote-specific product emerged. Claude's 200K context window makes multi-document comparison increasingly capable for tech-savvy users. Angi's ChatGPT integration (above) signals that AI chat is a distribution surface, not just a DIY tool — this space is heating up.

## Comparezy's Strategic Whitespace

No funded competitor owns these three positions — confirmed by latest research.

1. **Proposal evaluation gap** — Every platform stops at contractor connection or bid collection. No funded product helps homeowners evaluate what they've already received. Angi's ChatGPT integration actually reinforces this: they move homeowners from AI chat *to discovery*, not to decision-support. The moment after the quotes arrive is still wide open.

2. **Local cost benchmarking at point-of-decision** — HomeGuide updated their 2026 cost guides but still positions them pre-bid (budgeting phase). No one delivers "your quote is $X vs. the local median of $Y" at the moment a homeowner is holding 3 proposals. This is Comparezy's most defensible moat long-term — proprietary benchmark data from submitted proposals.

3. **Neutral negotiation coaching** — No platform tells homeowners what to push back on after receiving bids. Houzz's client dashboard upgrade shows homeowners want digital touchpoints during renovation, but it starts *after* contractor selection — not at the decision moment. Comparezy's negotiation checklist is differentiated and has no direct competitor.

---

## Priority Gaps to Close

1. **AI distribution strategy** — Angi embedding inside ChatGPT is a wake-up call. AI chat is a distribution channel. Comparezy should pursue a ChatGPT plugin or Claude integration that surfaces when homeowners ask about evaluating contractor quotes — before a lead-gen platform fills that slot. This is urgent.

2. **Cost benchmark data layer** — Even a Phase 1 version (sourced from HomeGuide ranges + RSMeans + Angi cost guides) would differentiate Comparezy immediately. Without it, Comparezy is "better AI chat" — with it, Comparezy is the only tool that tells you if your quote is fair.

3. **Shareable report quality and mobile UX** — Houzz's 2026 client dashboard research shows homeowners strongly want clean, digital, shareable project documentation (40%+ cite clearer timelines and communication as top needs). Comparezy's shareable report needs to be the best-looking output a homeowner can send to a spouse, parent, or advisor — on mobile, in under 60 seconds.
